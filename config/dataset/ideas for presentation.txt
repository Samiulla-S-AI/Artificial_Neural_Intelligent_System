2. **Cloud Dependency:**
   - Many AI systems rely on **cloud-based infrastructure** to process and store data. If the cloud servers are hacked, sensitive data can be stolen, leading to **massive data breaches**.
   - Example: In 2021, a major healthcare provider in India faced a data breach where **millions of patient records** were leaked due to vulnerabilities in their cloud storage.

3. **AI Model Vulnerabilities:**
   - AI models trained on sensitive data can be reverse-engineered or exploited to extract confidential information. For example, **adversarial attacks** can trick AI systems into revealing private data.
   - In finance, AI models used for fraud detection or credit scoring can be manipulated to expose customer data.

4. **Third-Party Risks:**
   - Many AI applications rely on third-party APIs or services, which can become weak points for data theft. If these third parties are compromised, the entire system becomes vulnerable.

5. **Lack of Regulation:**
